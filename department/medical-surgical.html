Medical-Surgical Nursing.

Adult acute care, peri-operative, critical care and specialty medical-surgical practice.

About the department

The Department of Medical-Surgical Nursing is responsible for teaching, research and clinical practice in the care of adult patients experiencing acute and complex illness. Faculty members teach across the undergraduate and postgraduate programmes and lead research programmes in medical, surgical, peri-operative and critical care nursing.

Teaching focus

  • Medical-Surgical Nursing I and II (undergraduate core)
  • Peri-operative Nursing (elective)
  • Critical Care Nursing (postgraduate)
  • Pharmacology for Nursing Practice
  • Clinical Skills and Objective Structured Clinical Examinations

Research themes

  • Post-operative recovery and enhanced recovery after surgery (ERAS)
  • Critical care outcomes and nurse-sensitive indicators
  • Chronic-disease self-management in adult populations
  • Wound care, infection prevention and pressure-injury prevention

Clinical partners

Clinical placements are hosted across the University Malaya Medical Centre and selected teaching hospitals in the Klang Valley — spanning general wards, surgical suites, high-dependency and intensive-care units.
