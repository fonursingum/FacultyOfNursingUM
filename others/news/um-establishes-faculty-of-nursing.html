UM sets up dedicated Faculty to address shortage of nurses.

Universiti Malaya takes a significant step forward in strengthening education and the development of nursing.

Universiti Malaya has established a dedicated Faculty of Nursing — a strategic response to Malaysia's growing need for nursing professionals and a milestone in strengthening the nation's healthcare system.

Officially established on 6 March 2026 following approval by the University Senate and Board of Directors, the Faculty is dedicated to producing highly-skilled, compassionate and future-ready nursing leaders for the nation. The move addresses a well-documented shortage of nurses in Malaysia and elevates the profession through rigorous academic and clinical training.

"The establishment of the Faculty of Nursing at Universiti Malaya is a strategic step to strengthen the nursing profession and produce highly-skilled, compassionate and future-ready nursing leaders for the nation."
— Prof. Dr. Hasniza Hashim, Vice-Chancellor

What this means

  • A dedicated academic home for nursing at Universiti Malaya, separate from the Faculty of Medicine
  • New Bachelor of Nursing Science and postgraduate pathways in development
  • Two founding departments: Medical-Surgical Nursing and Community & Mental Health Nursing
  • Strengthened clinical partnerships across the UM Medical Centre and teaching hospitals
