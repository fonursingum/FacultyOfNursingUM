Our history.

From a Senate proposal to Malaysia's newest nursing faculty — the founding chapter.

The Faculty of Nursing (Fakulti Kejururawatan) was formally established at Universiti Malaya on 6 March 2026 — the culmination of a coordinated proposal championed by university leadership and endorsed by both the Senate and the Board of Directors (Lembaga Pengarah Universiti, LPU).

The Faculty was created as a strategic, dedicated initiative to address Malaysia's critical shortage of nurses and to elevate the profession through rigorous academic and clinical training. The establishment marked a significant milestone in strengthening nursing academic development in the country.

26 February 2026

Senate approves the proposal

The Universiti Malaya Senate approves the proposal to establish the Faculty of Nursing (Fakulti Kejururawatan).

5 March 2026

Board of Directors endorses

The Board of Directors (LPU) considers and endorses the Senate's approval, welcoming the initiative as part of the University's push to strengthen academic development in nursing.

6 March 2026

Faculty officially established

The Faculty's establishment takes effect. Official names: Fakulti Kejururawatan (Bahasa Malaysia) and Faculty of Nursing (English).

9 March 2026

Formal notification issued

Professor Ir. Dr. Abdul Aziz bin Abdul Raman, Registrar (Pendaftar) of Universiti Malaya, issues the formal establishment letter (Pemakluman Penubuhan Fakulti Kejururawatan) to the university community.

27 March 2026

National coverage

The Star reports on the establishment as a national-level response to strengthening healthcare capacity and addressing the nursing shortage.

6 July 2026

Leadership Dialogue & Dean Selection

A Leadership Dialogue for the Dean Selection Exercise is convened at the Faculty of Medicine, moderated by the Registrar — a platform for candidates to share their vision for nursing excellence.
