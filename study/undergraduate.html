Bachelor's Degree

Bachelor of Nursing Science

The programme, offered by the Department of Nursing Sciences (established as a clinical department in 2007 and now under the newly-formed Faculty of Nursing), aims to produce "registered nurses that are competent, safe, has good moral values and critical thinking ability." The curriculum prepares students to practice nursing critically and ethically by applying scientific nursing foundations across the full spectrum of healthcare delivery.

Learning combines classroom instruction, simulation-based practice and immersive clinical placements from the earliest semesters, culminating in a supervised internship in the final year.

Programme structure

A four-year journey.

Y1

Foundations

Basic Medical Science, Fundamental Nursing, Nursing Skills, Microbiology, Ethics, Communication and Pharmacology.

Y2

Clinical Sciences

Medical-Surgical Nursing, Psychology & Sociology, Clinical Practice modules and electives.

Y3

Specialty rotations

Obstetric, Paediatric, Mental Health and Community Health Nursing, plus Research and advanced clinical practice.

Y4

Internship & leadership

Emergency & Intensive Care, Management, Research Project, Internship and professional practice modules.

Entry requirements

Who can apply.

Local applicants

STPM / Matriculation / Foundation / UM SATU

  • Malaysian citizen
  • Minimum grade B in two subjects: Biology and (Chemistry or Physics)
  • Minimum grade B in two SPM subjects: Science / Biology / Chemistry / Physics AND Mathematics / Additional Mathematics
  • Science stream enrolment

Diploma (CGPA ≥ 3.00)

  • Nursing Diploma from a recognised Malaysian institution
  • Meets the SPM subject requirements above

GCE A-Level

  • Minimum grade B in three subjects from Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Mathematics
  • Meets the SPM subject requirements above

International applicants

Senior High School Certificate

  • Minimum 65% average
  • 65% / grade B in Biology & Chemistry
  • 65% / grade B in Physics or Mathematics
  • GCSE O-Level or equivalent — minimum C in three relevant subjects

Diploma (CGPA ≥ 3.00)

  • Nursing Diploma from a recognised institution
  • 2 years clinical nursing experience
  • Professional registration in country of origin
  • Equivalent secondary qualifications

A-Level / IB (≥ 30 pts) / AUSMAT (≥ 80 ATAR)

  • Appropriate subject grades (grade B or equivalent)
  • Secondary qualification requirements

English language requirements

Local

MUET Band 3.0 — or conditional admission with IELTS 4.5 (Academic) or TOEFL iBT 39, provided MUET is completed within one academic session.

International

MUET 4.0 · IELTS (Academic) 6.0 · TOEFL iBT 60 · TOEFL Essentials 8.5 · PTE Academic 59 · or Cambridge qualifications (B1–C2, minimum 169).

Health & conduct. Applicants must be "certified to be in good health without any physical disabilities and mental illnesses" and free from serious infectious diseases or criminal convictions affecting clinical practice. Limited exceptions may apply for correctable physical conditions.
