Postgraduate studies.

Masters and doctoral programmes for nurses ready to specialise, lead, teach and research.

Master of Nursing Science (Advanced Practice)

A coursework-and-clinical Master's degree developing advanced practice nurses in medical-surgical, critical care, community and mental-health streams. Coursework, clinical practicum and a research-informed project prepare graduates for expanded clinical roles.

2 Years (Full-time)
Mode

Streams

Doctoral

Doctor of Philosophy (Nursing)

A research-intensive doctorate producing independent nursing scientists. Focus areas include clinical nursing, health systems, community health, mental-health nursing, education and policy.

Duration
3–5 Years
Mode
Research thesis
Award
PhD (Nursing)
Language
English

General entry requirements

  • Bachelor of Nursing (Hons) or equivalent recognised nursing degree
  • Current registration with the Malaysian Nursing Board (or country equivalent)
  • Minimum of one year post-registration clinical experience
  • IELTS 6.0 / TOEFL 550 or equivalent (for international applicants)
  • Research proposal (for research-track programmes)
